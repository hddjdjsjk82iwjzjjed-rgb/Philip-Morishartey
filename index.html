The GREAT Irish Famine
Those who don’t know… those who know.

The Great Irish Famine (1845–1852) was a period of mass starvation and disease in Ireland, caused by repeated potato crop failures due to blight. Millions of people were affected, leading to widespread death and emigration.

During this period, potatoes were the main food source for much of the population, and when crops failed, entire communities were left without reliable food supplies.

The Irish Republican Army (IRA) refers to a number of organisations in Irish history that were involved in the struggle for Irish independence from British rule. These groups played different roles at different times in Ireland’s political history.
